## How the last check was answered

The open question was whether a written rule survives contact with a real project, or whether
it needs `rotate.mjs` to be reliable. It was answered by migrating a live iOS repo end to end:
a 426-line `progress.md` and a 72-line `CLAUDE.md` split into hot files (274 lines, read every
session) and a cold archive (275 lines, grepped only). Every decision, lesson and piece of
evidence was placed by hand following `references/rotation.md`, and the audit confirmed the
result at 100/100 with no critical failures.

One qualifier worth keeping: this was one migration, performed carefully, by the author of the
rule. That is meaningfully weaker evidence than several sessions by different people. If future
sessions show rotation being skipped or half-done, `rotate.mjs` goes back on the table — the
audit check exists precisely so that failure would be visible rather than silent.

## Decisions

- Rotation stays a written rule, not a script. It is a cut-and-paste plus a link; the part that
  genuinely needs to be mechanical is the *enforcement*, which the audit provides.
- Write the archive file **before** removing the inline detail. The other order loses the
  evidence if the session is interrupted — this was caught the first time it was attempted here,
  when a regex silently failed and the audit stayed red until the rotation was actually complete.
